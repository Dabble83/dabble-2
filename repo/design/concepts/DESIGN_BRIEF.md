# Dabble — Design Brief

## Visual Identity
Dabble’s visual language is inspired by:
- Hand-drawn topo maps
- Travel journals
- Sketchbooks
- Soft watercolor illustrations

The tone should feel exploratory, human, and calm.
Not childish. Not corporate. Not bold “national park” branding.

---

## Core Aesthetic
- Soft paper-texture backgrounds
- Muted greens, blues, and warm neutrals
- Imperfect, organic shapes
- Simple symbolic illustrations instead of detailed drawings

Avoid:
- Avatars
- Emoji
- Stock SaaS iconography
- Hard edges or high contrast UI

---

## Illustration Guidelines
- Prefer simple shapes and outlines
- Hint at activities rather than fully illustrating them
- Use limited color palettes
- Favor symbolic representations over realism

Maps should feel like discovery tools, not navigation tools.

---

## Typography
- Clean, modern sans-serif for UI and body text
- Headings should feel friendly and readable
- No novelty or cartoon fonts

Typography should balance the illustrative elements by staying clean.

---

## Interaction Style
- Map-first exploration where possible
- Gentle hover states
- Subtle motion
- Nothing gamified or competitive

---

## Navigation Language
Use:
- Explore nearby
- Try something new
- Meet Dabblers
- Share what you know

Avoid:
- Hire
- Teach
- Book a lesson
- Experts

---
